Science vs. Authority​

Before modern science, people relied on authorities for "truth," whether that was the church, government, or respected elders. But these authorities are fallible, prone to biases, and sometimes have their own vested interests. The beauty of science lies in its cycle of observation, hypothesis, testing, and evaluation—a constant process of seeking truth with humility and skepticism.

The Power of Falsification​

Karl Popper introduced the concept of falsification: the idea that something is only scientific if it can be proven wrong. Science relies on this principle, allowing us to test hypotheses rigorously and reject theories when evidence contradicts them.
